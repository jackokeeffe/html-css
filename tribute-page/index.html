Steve Jobs

A pioneer of personal computers.

Steve Jobs presenting the first iPhone in 2007.

About:

"Steve Jobs was an American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media proprietor. He was the chairman, chief executive officer (CEO), and co-founder of Apple Inc., the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar, a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar, and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak." - Wikipedia

Timeline: (Source: CNET)

  • 1955: Steve Jobs is born and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1970: Steve Jobs meets future Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.
  • 1976: Co-founds Apple (with Wozniak & Ronald Wayne).
  • 1976: Apple I goes on sale at a price of US$666.66.
  • 1977: Releases the Apple II.
  • 1980: Apple goes public.
  • 1984: Apple releases the Macintosh for US$2500.
  • 1995: Steve Jobs becomes the president and CEO of Pixar.
  • 2001: Apple introduced iTunes.
  • 2001: The first Apple retail store opens.
  • 2001: Apple introduces the iPod.
  • 2007: Apple introduces the iPhone.
  • 2008: Apple introduces the App Store.
  • 2010: Apple introduces the iPad.
  • 2011: Steve Jobs passes away due to respiratory arrest, due to a tumor diagnosed in 2003.
